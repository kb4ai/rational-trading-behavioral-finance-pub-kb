# The 6-Layer "Inaction Privilege Stack" — Summary

*2026-03-13 — Key insight from cognitive science deep dive*

The brain privileges inaction through 6 separable, compounding mechanisms. Each layer independently biases toward holding; together they create a massive asymmetry.

```
L6 METACOGNITIVE BLINDSPOT
    Hold bypasses post-decisional monitoring.
    No "decision event" → no metacognitive evaluation trigger.
    Traders cannot assess whether their non-action was good or bad.
    (Fleming et al. 2012; Fenton-O'Creevy et al. 2003)

L5 ILLUSION OF CONTROL
    Passive monitoring reframed as active management.
    High illusion-of-control traders perform WORSE (N=107 professionals).
    (Langer 1975; Fenton-O'Creevy et al. 2003)

L4 TEMPORAL DISCOUNTING
    β ≈ 0.70–0.80: future rebalancing benefit discounted to irrelevance.
    Short-run: action regret > inaction regret.
    Long-run: reverses (but too late — damage done).
    Inaction cascades: missed opportunity compounds to next miss.
    (Laibson 1997; Gilovich & Medvec 1994; Tykocinski 1998)

L3 DUAL-PROCESS DEFAULT
    System 1 generates inaction heuristic automatically.
    System 2 override requires DLPFC engagement (metabolically costly).
    DLPFC activation correlates r = −0.39 with omission bias reduction.
    High-CRT individuals show reduced omission bias.
    (Frederick 2005; Cushman et al. 2006, 2011)

L2 ATTENTIONAL SELECTIVITY
    9.5% fewer portfolio logins after market declines (ostrich effect).
    Attention avoidance precisely when rebalancing OC is highest.
    Inattentional blindness: position drift missed under cognitive load.
    (Karlsson et al. 2009; Sicherman et al. 2016; Simons & Chabris 1999)

L1 NEURAL ASYMMETRY
    Anterior insula: encodes "pain of commission" but NOT "pain of omission."
    Paper losses bypass insula circuit → feel less painful than realized losses.
    OFC/amygdala accumulate regret-aversion across trials → progressive
    action suppression.
    No motor affordance for "hold" → no felt sense of agency.
    (Greene et al. 2001; Knutson et al. 2007; Camille et al. 2004;
     Coricelli et al. 2005; Nicolle et al. 2011; Cisek & Kalaska 2010)
```

## Debiasing Must Target Specific Layers

| Layer | Intervention | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| L1 Neural | Algorithmic execution | Bypass neural circuit entirely |
| L2 Attention | Mandatory review schedule; inverse-VIX attention | Counter ostrich effect |
| L3 Dual-process | Checklists, decision logs | Force System 2 engagement |
| L4 Temporal | Pre-commitment, auto-rebalancing | Remove future-self negotiation |
| L5 Control | Track actual decision accuracy vs perceived | Calibrate metacognition |
| L6 Metacognitive | Inaction justification log | Create "decision event" for holds |

**Key insight for the KB**: A single debiasing intervention is insufficient because the 6 layers are separable and additive. Effective debiasing requires stacking interventions across all layers — exactly what the [Symmetric Action-Inaction Evaluator](../../debiasing-frameworks/symmetric-action-inaction-evaluator.md) aims to do.
